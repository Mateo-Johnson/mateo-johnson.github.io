Hi, I'm Mateo Johnson

I'm a dual enrollment high school and college student, as well as an aspiring engineer with a strong passion for robotics, control systems, embedded systems and avionics. I’ve led my school’s robotics team to multiple victories and am always seeking opportunities to expand my knowledge.

Beyond robotics, I enjoy creating open-source software like my Glimmer website builder, and teaching young students about the wonders of STEM.

My Journey

2014

Introduction to STEM

My grandfather fostered my love of computer science and engineering as best as he could, teaching me about mechanical, electrical and computer engineering.

2018

Learned First Language

I learned Arduino specific C++, which then eventually led to normal C++. These languages were mainly used for Arduino programming, in addition to other similar microntrollers.

2019

Started Community Outreach

Began mentoring younger students in robotics and computer science, organizing workshops to inspire the next generation of engineers.

2020

Learned 3-4 more languages

Once the US went into lockdown in early 2020, I had so much time on my hands, that I decided it would be worthwhile to learn Python, Java, HTML, CSS and JS during the time I was not doing schoolwork.

2022

Joined My Schools Robotics Team

I joined my schools robotics team, The Metrobots. I joined as a programming manager due to prior experience, but helped with other subgroups too.

Skills & Experience

Throughout my journey, I’ve developed a wide array of skills in various fields of engineering and technology. For more detail please visit my Skills Page. Here is an overview of the key areas I focus on:


Robotics

Programming, building, and testing robots. I enjoy working with both electrical and mechanical engineering, as well as the computer science part. I wrote the code for a Swerve Drive drivetrain fully from scratch.

Software Development

Web development, apps, and open-source tools. I have made tools for my FRC team, Embold (The company I interned for) and personal use.

Embedded Systems

Working with microcontrollers and sensors, ranging from Arduinos to more complicated FPGAs, even making my own.

STEM Outreach

In my time off of school, I enjoy mentoring and teaching younger students. I enjpoy spreading STEM to future generations.

CAD and EDA

Using Onshape and Fusion360 for CAD design, along with LTSpice, EasyEDA and KiCAD for EDA, I enjoy making boards and models for personal and other projects.
